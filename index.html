Accommodation

We generally recommend staying near TP around Bedok, Tampines, Pasir Ris.

Pricing for HDB Rooms ranges from 600-1000 SGD per month.

Public Transport

Singapore has a great public transport system, you can plot your journey through mobile apps Google Maps or Moovit for the best experience.

Polytechnic students can use the Diploma Concession Card which gives you half the fare price of normal adult fares on public transport.

Diploma Concession Cards have a monthly 25SGD (Bus), 30SGD (MRT) or 50SGD (MRT + Bus) passes.

However depending on your usage, the monthly pass may not be worth it.

Student Pass

You will most likely be entering Singapore with your IPA Letter, after which you will need to head to ICA and get your full Student’s pass which will be valid for 3 years.

Working as a Student

Students are allowed to work but there are limits to it.

During the school term: 16 hours per week
(with exception to internship in Year 3)

During the school vacation: No limit

Typical hour rates ranges from 7-8 SGD/hour

You can try looking for jobs on these websites, or go through an agency (pls help)

Mobile Plans

Singtel, StarHub, M1
These are primary vendors but generally not worth it if you prefer data over text/calls.

Gomo (SingTel), Giga (StarHub), Circles (M1 Network), TPG
These are vendors who uses the above mentioned providers’s lines (with exception of TPG), more focused on data usage and less on text/calls. You do need to be over 18 (or with a guardian’s approval) for these vendors.

As a general guide, Singtel and StarHub has the best service coverage, with M1 and TPG having sometimes less ideal coverage.

Banks

DBS/POSB (they’re the same), OCBC, UOB are the major banks of Singapore.

DBS/POSB are the most popular with the most ATMs and users.
OCBC has their OCBC Frank student account and debit card.
UOB and OCBC ATMs can be used interchangeably with each other.

There is an OCBC ATM inside TP at the end of School of Business.
